## Key Strategic Principles

### 1. Best Player Available > Positional Need (Sim-Validated)
Simulations show best-value wins 99.5% of the time vs positional-need. Don't reach for positions — the value gap is $74+. Trust the board.

### 2. SP ADP Arbitrage Is Your Biggest Edge
The system sees massive mispricing in SP. These players are drafted 60-100+ picks earlier by value than by ADP:
- **Sonny Gray** (+102 picks): ADP ~103, value $39.6 — R1 pick in sims, R9 by ADP
- **Brandon Woodruff** (+106): ADP ~113, value $29.6 — R4 in sims
- **Framber Valdez** (+70): ADP ~76, value $36.4 — R3 in sims
- **Shane McClanahan** (+204): ADP ~217, value $19.4 — R6 in sims
- **Ketel Marte** (+25): ADP ~27, value $43.1 — best non-SP arbitrage

### 3. Fill Positions Cheap and Late
The sims fill 1B (Schanuel, R12), 2B (Edwards, R10), C (Bailey, R21), and OF (Profar/Marsee, R8-9) all in the back half. Positional drop-offs matter less than SP value gaps. Don't panic about 1B/C scarcity — serviceable options last deep.

### 4. Build a Pitching Fortress
With Sánchez + Díaz already kept, adding Gray + Webb + Valdez + Woodruff + Castillo + McClanahan creates a dominant pitching staff. You'd own ERA, WHIP, SO, W, and SV+HLD — that's 5 of 10 categories locked before your bats even contribute.

### 5. Don't Chase SB — You Already Win It
Witt + Chisholm make you the SB leader. Don't waste draft capital on speed-only players.

### 6. OBP Remains Your Biggest Batting Gap
At 11th of 12 in keeper OBP, grab high-OBP bats when the value is right — but don't reach:
- Marte (.350+ OBP) — best value bat in the draft
- Olson (.350+ OBP) — fills 1B need too
- Profar, Edwards — cheaper OBP contributors available in rounds 8-10
